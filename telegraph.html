The 16 best Christmas crackers of 2023, from luxury crackers to traditional choices

Tried and tested at home, here are the best Christmas crackers of 2023, from M&S to Hotel Chocolat, with not a plastic moustache in sight

Jessica Salter 21 December 2023 11:07am
This article contains affiliate links. The products or services listed have been selected independently by journalists after hands-on testing or sourcing expert opinions. We may earn a commission when you click a link, buy a product or subscribe to a service.

The best Christmas cracker is the one that brings your Christmas table settings to life. Formal, silly, vintage, luxury or traditional, there are styles to suit all tastes and budgets and this year manufacturers have really upped their game. But which is the right one for you?

Crackers containing games can be a fun choice. This year Marks & Spencers range includes crackers with charades and other family games inside. Crackers containing foodie treats such as those from Melt are easy crowd pleasers, while it pays to give children crackers with a treat worth keeping. Meri Meris cracker treats this year make lovely bedroom decorations in their own right.

Where crackers used to be extremely eco-unfriendly, this year that is not the case. The best crackers Ive found tend to be glitter-free (or to use eco glitter) and recyclable except for the bows, which can be reused. The gifts inside them are no longer plastic rubbish, but things you can use.

Ive reviewed all this years best crackers and you can read my reviews below, followed by a guide to how to make your own (its quite easy). If youre in a hurry, heres a quick look at my top five:

Which are the best Christmas crackers of 2023? At a glance

  • Best overall: Dunelm Luxury Navy Star Crackers
  • Best Swarovski Christmas crackers: Clear Crystal Luxury Christmas Crackers
  • Best luxury Christmas crackers: Harvey Nichols Luxurious Christmas Crackers
  • Best beauty crackers: LOccitane Festive Crackers collection
  • Best charity Christmas crackers: Shelter Fill Your Own Christmas Crackers

How I chose the best crackers

Ive only reviewed crackers that are made from recyclable, plastic-free materials, with no plastic tat inside. To be fair to the retailers, that is becoming more and more popular each year. And to ensure that they truly are crowd pleasers, I roped in my husband and two children to help make sure they come with their approval, too.

There is a huge variety of crackers this year, with gifts to suit children, beauty lovers, chocoholics or perhaps those who just want a bit of retro Christmas cheer. Unbelievably you can spend as much as 1,000. I personally think youd be crackers to waste such a large amount of money on a temporary thrill. So for the purposes of this guide Ive mostly kept it to under 50, with exceptions made for some very popular high-end crackers, and some that you can reuse each year, bringing down the cost per use over time.

If youre preparing for Christmas, you may also want to read my guide to the best artificial Christmas trees, Simon Lewiss guide to the best advent calendars and Rebecca Astills guides to the best beauty advent calendars and best Christmas hampers.

Christmas Cracker FAQ

Who invented Christmas crackers?

A London confectioner called Tom Smith in the 1840s, according to the Victoria & Albert Museum which has some of the earliest examples in its collection. He took the French idea of bonbons wrapped in paper, which were popular at Christmas, and added two thin strips of paper with silver fulminate painted on one and an abrasive surface on the other. When pulled apart, the friction caused the silver fulminate to explode with a crack.

Rather than a joke, Smith included a love motto inside each cracker. It was his son Walter who added the novelty gifts and tissue paper crowns, which for decades were made by hand; by the 1890s the company employed 2,000 people.

How to make your own Christmas crackers

All you need is a kitchen roll tube, some decorative paper, some ribbon and a cracker snap (2.30 for 24 on Amazon).

  1. Cut your kitchen roll into one long cylinder (for the body) and two short ones (for the handles)
  2. Where you have just cut, create zigzag teeth along the edges. This will make the cracker easier to assemble
  3. Put your paper crown (5.48 for 100 on Amazon), cracker jokes (from 1.49 on eBay) and gift into the long cylinder and thread the cracker snap through it
  4. Wrap all three parts of the kitchen roll tube in decorative paper, with the zigzag teeth touching and the cracker snap protruding at both ends. Sellotape the paper together. Use the ribbon to cinch it where the zigzag teeth are, to create the handles. Trim each end and youre done
  5. Instead of using decorative paper, why not get the kids to decorate it themselves? Or write guests names and personalise the gifts inside to that person
